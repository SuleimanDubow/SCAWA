OUR SERVICES

  1. Raise Fund for Healthy Food

    We aim to combat malnutrition by raising funds to provide healthy and nutritious food to those in need, ensuring that every child and woman has access to balanced meals.

  2. Education for Poor Children

    Our educational programs focus on providing access to quality education for underprivileged children, helping them to achieve their academic goals and break the cycle of poverty.

  3. Promoting the Rights of Children

    We advocate for the rights of children, ensuring they are protected from abuse and exploitation, and have access to opportunities that allow them to thrive.

  4. Massive Donation to the Poor

    We organize donation drives to collect essential items such as clothing, food, and school supplies, distributing them to families and individuals in need.

  5. Huge Help to Homeless Pupils

    We provide support to homeless children, offering them safe shelter, educational resources, and emotional support to help them rebuild their lives.

  1. Let's Build Some Homes

    Our housing projects focus on building safe and affordable homes for families, creating stable living environments where children can grow and thrive.

  2. Pure Water for Poor People

    Access to clean water is a basic human right. We work on providing pure water to communities lacking this essential resource, improving their health and quality of life.

  3. Medical Facilities

    We offer medical services and facilities to ensure that everyone, especially women and children, receives the healthcare they need, including vaccinations, check-ups, and emergency care.

  4. Women's Health and Empowerment

    We focus on women's health by providing access to reproductive health services, maternal care, and empowerment programs that equip women with the skills and knowledge to lead healthier, more independent lives.

  5. Support for Single Mothers

    We offer support programs for single mothers, helping them with childcare, job training, and financial assistance, ensuring they have the resources needed to provide for their families.

In Somalia, where poverty and conflict continue to pose significant challenges, the Somali Children and Women Advocacy (SCAWA) emerges as a symbol of hope and perseverance. Our mission is to champion human rights, save lives, and improve the conditions of the most vulnerable, especially women and children. We are driven by our belief in the inherent potential of every person, irrespective of their circumstances.

Our approach to empowerment is both comprehensive and diverse. We recognize that meeting the needs of at-risk communities requires more than temporary aid; it necessitates sustainable solutions that address the root causes of poverty and inequality. Through our various programs, we strive to create lasting change that not only alleviates immediate suffering but also fosters self-reliance and dignity.

Education is a key focus of our work. In many areas of Somalia, quality education remains out of reach for many children, particularly girls. SCAWA is committed to overcoming these obstacles by providing inclusive and equitable educational opportunities. We offer scholarships, build schools, and train teachers to ensure every child has the chance to learn and thrive.

Healthcare is another critical area of our work. We believe that access to healthcare is a fundamental right for all. Our health initiatives aim to enhance maternal and child health, combat malnutrition, and provide essential medical services. We operate clinics and mobile health units in remote regions, ensuring that even the most isolated communities receive necessary care.

Economic empowerment is also a key aspect of our strategy. By providing vocational training and supporting small businesses, we help individuals acquire the skills and resources needed to generate income and improve their livelihoods. This not only promotes personal development but also strengthens the local economy and builds community resilience.

Our water, sanitation, and hygiene (WASH) programs are essential for providing communities with access to clean water and proper sanitation facilities. These initiatives aim to prevent disease spread and improve overall health outcomes. We focus on constructing wells, latrines, and promoting hygiene education to foster healthier living environments.

Amid ongoing conflicts and displacement, SCAWA offers critical support to internally displaced persons (IDPs) and refugees. Our emergency relief efforts include food distribution, shelter, and psychosocial support. We address immediate needs while working towards long-term solutions that promote stability and security.

Community engagement and participation are fundamental to our approach. We believe that sustainable change is achieved when local communities are actively involved in decision-making. By collaborating with community leaders and members, we ensure that our programs are culturally relevant and effectively address each communityâ€™s unique needs.

Our advocacy efforts aim to shape policies and practices at both local and national levels. We work to raise awareness about human rights issues, promote gender equality, and support legislation that benefits vulnerable populations. Through advocacy, we seek to create an environment where everyone can fully enjoy their rights and opportunities.

Partnerships are vital to enhancing our impact. We collaborate with other NGOs, government agencies, and international organizations to pool resources, share expertise, and coordinate efforts. These partnerships enable us to reach a broader audience and implement our programs more effectively.

SMART CHARITABLES

Somali Children and Women Advocacy (SCAWA) is an independent, non-governmental, non-profit and non political organization established to advocate for the protection of human rights, save and improve lives of most vulnerable, poorest and marginalized families affected by poverty and/or crisis, eradicate poverty and empower women and children to reach their full potential.
